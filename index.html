How I got here

From Algeria to Paris to San Francisco. Every step built on the last one.

Algeria → Paris

I've been obsessed with technology since I was a kid.

I grew up in Algeria taking apart every piece of tech I could get my hands on. That curiosity brought me to France, where I pursued a Master's in Entrepreneurship and Project Management at Paris Nanterre.

I didn't wait for the diploma. While still studying, I founded my first company, Maison Salomon, at Station F, Europe's largest startup campus. We helped seniors adopt technology. When COVID hit, that work became critical: for many isolated people, a screen was the only way to see their family. I trained dozens of them.

I also worked in sales every weekend to finance my studies. That combination, building a startup while studying while selling, taught me more than any class ever could.

2021

I discovered the EV charging industry. Everything clicked.

I did drone inspections on construction sites for groups like Bouygues, then joined Rexel, Europe's largest electrical distributor. My mission: drive across France and train 350+ B2B clients across 35 agencies on EV charging solutions. I put 10,000+ km on the car covering Paris, Brittany, and Auvergne-Rhone-Alpes.

That's when I realized the EV infrastructure space was massive, early, and badly needed people who could bridge the gap between technical and business. I decided to go all in.

2022 → 2023

I led some of the biggest EV deployments in France.

Sicame Academy brought me on to train 100+ professional electricians on P1/P2/P3 charging standards. Major brands, Schneider, Hager, ABB, Legrand, Wallbox, EVBox, sent their teams to my sessions. I became a certified EVSE Expert.

At AutoPlug, I helped build a connected charger display that was showcased at the Paris Auto Show 2022 alongside Ford and BYD, with significant media coverage.

Then the major projects came in:

  • Carrefour Property: 115 supermarket sites, ultra-fast chargers, as part of the Paris 2024 Olympics partnership.
  • McDonald's (via Resonance / Groupe Firalp): 73 fast-charging stations at 300 kW. First restaurant chain in France to go electric. I created a standardization system that reduced deployment variables from 22 to 2.
  • Tesla: Supercharger V3 site, civil and electrical works.
  • Fastned: Site coordination for their ultra-fast charging hubs.
2024

I took on the hardest challenge in EV: heavy vehicles.

At Altens, I built the company's entire heavy-vehicle charging strategy from scratch. The "Pur Elec" offer. I negotiated with 18+ major DC/AC manufacturers, assembled a national installer network, and directed 17 projects for clients like Heineken, Scania, and Renault Trucks operating 24/7 logistics platforms.

Light vehicles, heavy vehicles, retail, logistics, highway, underground. I'd deployed charging infrastructure in every context that existed. The question became: what's next?

2025 → now

San Francisco. Robotics. Chargebotic.

I moved to San Francisco to sharpen my technical and go-to-market skills. I built TomFeed (content and personal branding), shipped a consumer mobile app, enrolled in Stanford's Human-Centered AI program, and launched a 30 apps challenge to build in public.

But the real insight was this: after years of solving "how do you charge vehicles at scale," I saw the same unsolved problem emerging in robotics. Delivery robots, warehouse robots, agricultural robots, and eventually lunar rovers. They all need autonomous power. Nobody has cracked it yet.

That's why I founded Chargebotic. Autonomous charging infrastructure for robots, on Earth and beyond. Everything I've built over 7+ years, deploying 1,000+ chargers, managing complex multi-stakeholder projects, negotiating with hardware manufacturers, training hundreds of professionals, it all leads here.
